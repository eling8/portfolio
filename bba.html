Rube Goldberg Machine

As a class, we built a single large Rube Goldberg Machine. Our group was responsible for the final step of the sequence; our machine was activated by infrared light and ended with the creation of an artistic, painted sign for the class.

  • An infrared sensor activates a servo motor, which rotates 3D-printed cups full of paint so that the paint spills onto the wooden sign below.
  • Prototyped several paint-tipping mechanisms, including using gears, rolling balls, and various ways of attaching the paint cans.
  • Sensor and motor are activated using a GoGo board.
  • Designed and laser-cut the stand, sign, and motor setup.

Additional information

This is a collection of various small projects done for Beyond Bits And Atoms: Designing Technological Tools (CS402/EDUC211), which I took during winter 2019 at Stanford University. The course focused on designing and prototyping educational technologies, with course material on both pedagogical frameworks as well as various fabrication techniques.

The Dream Toy project and Rube Goldberg project were done in partnership with two other students, where I played significant roles in developing the project concept and did the bulk of the laser cutting design work. The remaining work is entirely my own.
